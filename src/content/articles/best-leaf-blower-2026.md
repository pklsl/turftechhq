A leaf blower is one of those tools that seems unnecessary until you have one — and then becomes indispensable. Whether it's clearing the driveway in 5 minutes instead of 30, cleaning out gutters, blowing grass clippings off hard surfaces, or wrangling 300 cubic feet of fall leaves into a pile, a good leaf blower makes the work dramatically faster.

But "leaf blower" encompasses an enormous range of tools: from a $59 corded electric barely powerful enough to move dry leaves, to a $600 professional backpack blower that could strip bark off a tree. Choosing wrong means either a tool that can't handle your workload, or an expensive, heavy machine you don't need.

This guide reviews the best leaf blowers of 2026 across all types and price points, with a practical framework for choosing the right one for your property.

## Types of Leaf Blowers: Pros and Cons

### Handheld Gas Leaf Blowers

Gas-powered handheld blowers run on 2-cycle gasoline engines. They deliver strong CFM and MPH figures and work anywhere without batteries or cords.

**Pros:** High power, no battery management, works anywhere
**Cons:** Loud, heavy relative to output, exhaust fumes, 2-cycle maintenance, increasingly restricted in some municipalities

**Best for:** Medium-large properties; users not wanting to invest in a battery platform

### Handheld Battery Leaf Blowers

**The dominant choice for residential use in 2026.** Modern 40V–80V+ battery blowers now match or exceed mid-range gas models in CFM output, with significantly less noise, weight, and maintenance.

**Pros:** Quiet, no fumes, instant start, lighter than gas, battery platform compatibility
**Cons:** Limited runtime, battery cost if not already in an ecosystem

**Best for:** Most residential applications; anyone already in a major battery platform ecosystem

### Corded Electric Leaf Blowers

**Pros:** Consistent power, lightweight, lowest price
**Cons:** Limited by cord length, awkward cord management, not practical for large yards

**Best for:** Small, simple properties; garage/workshop cleaning; budget buyers

### Backpack Gas Leaf Blowers

**The professional standard.** Backpack blowers shift weight to your back and core — dramatically more comfortable than handheld models on long sessions. Gas backpacks produce the highest CFM figures available to homeowners.

**Pros:** Maximum power, best ergonomics for heavy work, professional-grade
**Cons:** Expensive, loud, requires 2-cycle fuel mix, overkill for small properties

**Best for:** Large properties with heavy leaf loads; professional landscapers; 1+ acre properties

### Backpack Battery Leaf Blowers

The newest and fastest-growing category. Premium 80V+ backpack battery blowers are closing the gap on gas significantly.

**Pros:** Gas-competing power with battery convenience, quieter, no fumes
**Cons:** Very expensive, long charge times, battery weight is significant

**Best for:** Large properties where you want gas power but without fumes and noise

### Walk-Behind Leaf Blowers

Massive wheeled units for commercial use — beyond the scope of most homeowners. Mentioned for completeness.

## CFM vs. MPH: What Actually Matters

This is the most confusing aspect of leaf blower shopping, and manufacturers exploit the confusion constantly.

**CFM (Cubic Feet per Minute)** measures the volume of air the blower moves. More CFM = more air mass being moved.

**MPH (Miles Per Hour)** measures the velocity of the air exiting the nozzle. More MPH = air exits faster.

**Which matters more?** For most leaf-moving tasks, **CFM is more important**. High CFM means you can move larger volumes of wet, heavy material. High MPH on low CFM just creates a narrow, fast jet that isn't great at moving leaf piles.

For context:
- **Under 300 CFM:** Light duty — dry leaves on smooth surfaces only
- **300–500 CFM:** Good residential performance — most conditions
- **500–700 CFM:** Strong residential/light commercial — wet leaves, debris piles
- **700+ CFM:** Professional/heavy commercial use

## Leaf Blower Buying Guide

### Matching Blower Power to Property Size

| Property/Task | Recommended CFM |
|---|---|
| Small patio/driveway | 200–350 CFM |
| Small lawn under 0.25 acres | 300–450 CFM |
| Medium lawn 0.25–0.75 acres | 450–600 CFM |
| Large lawn 0.75–2 acres | 550–700 CFM |
| 2+ acres / heavy fall cleanup | 700+ CFM (backpack recommended) |

### Gas vs. Battery Decision Framework

**Choose battery if:**
- Your property is under 1 acre
- You're already in a major battery platform (EGO, DEWALT, etc.)
- Noise or emissions are a concern

**Choose gas (backpack) if:**
- You have 1.5+ acres with heavy fall leaf loads
- You want maximum power regardless of price
- You need multi-hour runtime without stopping

### Understanding Noise Levels

Leaf blowers are among the loudest residential power tools:
- **Corded electric:** 65–70 dB (normal conversation level)
- **Battery handheld:** 65–75 dB
- **Gas handheld:** 90–100 dB (hearing protection recommended)
- **Gas backpack:** 105–115 dB (hearing protection required)

Many municipalities now have noise ordinances that effectively restrict gas blowers during certain hours or altogether. Check local regulations before buying.
